Aurral Pocket Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 21, 2026

Aurral Pocket ("the app", "we", "us") is an open-source mobile client for Aurral, a self-hosted music discovery and request manager for Lidarr. The app is published by Dylan Francis and distributed through the Apple App Store and Google Play. The source code is available at github.com/dylfrancis/aurral-pocket.

This policy explains what data the app handles, where that data goes, and what choices you have. The short version: the app does not operate any servers of its own, does not run analytics or tracking, and does not send your data to us or to any third party. It only talks to the Aurral server that you configure.

Summary. Aurral Pocket connects only to the self-hosted Aurral server address you enter. Credentials, your session token, and app preferences are stored on your device. No analytics, telemetry, advertising SDKs, or third-party trackers are included.

1. About the Aurral ecosystem

Aurral Pocket is a client; it is not a service. All music discovery, library browsing, search, and request management is performed by the Aurral server instance you run yourself (typically via Docker) and that you connect the app to by entering its URL.

Because Aurral is self-hosted, the operator of the Aurral server - not the authors of Aurral Pocket - controls how data on that server is stored, processed, and shared with any upstream services it integrates with (for example MusicBrainz, Last.fm, Lidarr, Navidrome). If you are connecting to someone else's Aurral server, ask them about their practices. If you are running your own, you are the operator.

2. Information the app handles

2.1 Information you provide

  • Server address. The URL of the Aurral server you want the app to connect to.
  • Account credentials. Your Aurral username and password, entered during sign-in to obtain a session token from your server.
  • Optional ZIP / postal code. If you use the "Shows near you" discovery section and choose to enter a postal code instead of IP-based location, that value is stored on your device and sent to your Aurral server with nearby-show requests.
  • Search queries and interactions you submit while using the app (artist searches, requests, etc.) - these are sent to your Aurral server so it can respond to them.

2.2 Information stored on your device

The following is stored locally on your device. None of it is transmitted to the authors of Aurral Pocket.

  • Secure storage (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore, via expo-secure-store):
    • Session token issued by your Aurral server
    • Cached profile info returned by your server (user id, username, role)
    • Session expiry and last-active timestamps
    • Your username and password - only if you explicitly enable "Remember me"
    • Your biometric (Face ID / Touch ID) preference
  • Local app storage (via AsyncStorage):
    • Your configured Aurral server URL
    • Recent search history (kept on-device; you can clear it from the search screen)
    • Nearby-location preference (IP-based or a ZIP / postal code you entered)

You can remove all of this data at any time by signing out and/or uninstalling the app.

2.3 Information automatically collected

None by the app. Aurral Pocket does not embed any analytics, crash reporting, advertising, attribution, or tracking SDKs. It does not collect device identifiers, IP addresses, contacts, calendars, photos, audio, health data, or precise GPS location.

Apple and Google independently collect aggregate install and crash information for apps distributed through the App Store and Google Play. That data is governed by Apple's privacy policy and Google's privacy policy, respectively, and the app authors do not have access to personally identifiable information from it.

3. How information is used

  • The server URL, token, and credentials are used solely to authenticate and communicate with the Aurral server you configured.
  • Recent searches and nearby-location preferences are used only to populate UI on your device.
  • Biometric authentication (Face ID / Touch ID) is used only to re-unlock a saved session on your device. Biometric data itself never leaves your device - iOS and Android handle the match and only return a yes/no result to the app.

4. Network connections

The app makes network requests to:

  • Your Aurral server, at the URL you provide, for authentication, discovery, library, search, requests, and album artwork.
  • Any hosts that your Aurral server redirects image URLs to (for example, cover art hosted by your server or linked from upstream music metadata providers). The app does not choose these hosts; it only renders URLs returned by your server.

The app does not contact any servers operated by the authors of Aurral Pocket, and does not contact any third-party analytics or advertising networks.

5. Device permissions

  • Face ID / Touch ID / biometrics (optional). Used only to re-authenticate an existing session. You can decline this permission and continue using password sign-in.
  • Microphone. Explicitly not requested. The Android manifest blocks RECORD_AUDIO.
  • Location. The app does not request device location. The "Shows near you" feature uses IP-based geolocation performed by your Aurral server, or a postal code you type in - never GPS.
  • Contacts, Photos, Calendar, Health, Motion. Not requested.

6. Data sharing

The app authors do not receive, store, or share your data. The only party that receives data from the app is the Aurral server whose address you enter. How that server uses or forwards data (for example, to Lidarr, Last.fm, MusicBrainz, or Navidrome) is determined by its operator and configuration, not by the app.

7. Data retention and deletion

  • On-device data persists until you sign out, clear it from the app, or uninstall the app. Uninstalling removes all app-stored data from the device.
  • Data on your Aurral server is retained according to the operator's configuration. If you run your own server, you control retention. If you use someone else's, contact that operator.

8. Children's privacy

Aurral Pocket is not directed to children under 13, and the authors do not knowingly collect information from them - in fact, the authors collect no information from any user. Parents or guardians who believe a child's information may have been stored on an Aurral server should contact that server's operator.

9. Security

Sensitive values (session token, saved credentials if you enable "Remember me", session metadata) are stored using the operating system's secure keystore. Traffic between the app and your Aurral server is only as secure as the address you configure; we strongly recommend using HTTPS.

No system is perfectly secure. You are responsible for keeping the device on which the app is installed physically secure and up to date.

10. Your choices

  • Sign out from Settings to clear the session token on the device.
  • Disable "Remember me" and biometric sign-in from the sign-in screen to avoid storing credentials on the device.
  • Clear recent searches from the search screen.
  • Uninstall the app to remove all app-stored data from your device.

11. Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated from time to time. Changes are published at the same URL as this document, with a revised "Last updated" date. The version history is also visible in the project repository on GitHub.
